Normal Eye movement, conjugate eye deviation, and lone abducting eye
Nicholas Woolridge

This illustration is meant to explain a new theory on the coordination of eye movements (involving one-way traffic in the corpus callosum), and its clinical implications for explaining anomalous eye movements in some cases of lateralized cerebral ischemia. Illustration by Nicholas Woolridge and Lianne Friesen.

From In How do the eyes move together? New understandings help explain eye deviations in patients with stroke. Derakhshan I. Canadian Medical Association Journal, Jan 2005; 172: 171 - 173; 10.1503/Canadian Medical Association Journal.1040322.
